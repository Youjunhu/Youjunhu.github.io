Whether the 1∕2π factor appears at the forward transformation or inverse one is actually a matter of convention. The only requirement is that the product of the two factors in the forward and inverse transformation is equal to 1∕2π. To obtain a more symmetric pair, one can adopt a factor 1∕ at both the forward and inverse transformation. The representation in Eqs. (34) and (35) is adopted in this note. This important question is answered by the famous sampling theorem, which sates that a suitable Δ to make Eq. (38) exact is given by Δ ≤ 1∕(2fc), where fc is the largest frequency contained in h(t) (i.e., H(f) = 0 for |f| > fc).
