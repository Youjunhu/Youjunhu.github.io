(53) is usually an approximation. Do we have some rules to choose a suitable Δ so that Eq. (53) can become a good approximation or even an exact relation? This important question is answered by the sampling theorem, which sates that a suitable Δ to make Eq. (53) exact is given by Δ ≤ 1∕(2fc), where fc is the largest frequency contained in h(t) (i.e., H(f) = 0 for |f| > fc).
