As is conventionally adopted in multivariables calculus, both fp and fg are sometimes simply denoted by f. Which one is actually assumed depends on the context, i.e., depends on which independent variables are actually assumed: particle variables or guiding-center variables. This is one of the subtle (trivial?) things needed to be noted for gyrokinetic theory in particular and for multivariables calculus in general. This notation will cause confusions when, for example, fg(X,v) is evaluated at X = x, i.e., fg(x,v), which, if the subscript g is omitted and we rely on dependent variables to identify whether it is fp or fg, will be wrongly understood as fp. Therefore it seems better to use the accurate notation. One example where this distinguishing is important is encountered when we try to express the diamagnetic Ô¨Çow in terms of fg, which is discussed in Appendix A.
