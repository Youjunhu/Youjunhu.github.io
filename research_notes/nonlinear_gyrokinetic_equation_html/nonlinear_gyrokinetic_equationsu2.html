2.2 Gyro-angle

The forward and backward guiding-center transformations (2) and (4) involve the velocity vector v. It is the cross product between v and e∥(x) or e∥(X) that is actually used. Therefore, only two of the three coordinates of v is actually needed in a local (at x or X) cylindrical coordinate system, namely v⊥ and α, where α is the azimuthal angle of the velocity in the local cylindrical system. Here α will be called the gyro-phase or gyro-angle in the following.
