If neglecting the magnetic drift, a guiding-center orbit is along a magnetic Ô¨Åeld lines, i.e., there is no derivation from the magnetic surface where a guiding center is initially located. Taking the magnetic drift into account, a guiding-center orbit will deviate from the initial magnetic surface, giving an orbit of nozero width in the poloidal plane.

Whether a guiding-center will drift radially outward or inward from a local magnetic surface near the midplane can be determined in the following way. First note that the zero-order approximation of the guiding-center orbit (zero-width orbit) is either parallel or anti-parallel to the local magnetic Ô¨Åeld, depending on the sign of v‚à•. Finally, by imposing the magnetic drift on the zero-width orbit, we can determine whether the guiding center will drift inward or outward from the local magnetic surface. Examining the above results, one Ô¨Ånds that reversing the direction of the toroidal magnetic Ô¨Åeld does not change the projection of orbits on the poloidal plane, i.e., the location and shape of the poloidal orbits remain the same. However the direction of the poloidal motion is changed from clockwise (anti-clockwise) to anti-clockwise (clockwise). Examining the above results, we can also Ô¨Ånd that, for particles launched from low-Ô¨Åeld-side midplane, co-current partilces have their orbits inside the magnetic surface at which the particle is iniitally located, and counter-current particles have thier orbits outside of the magnetic surface. These conclusions have important implications for the neutral beam injection, where orbits outside a reference magnetic surface (birth location) are more likely to be lost to the wall of the machine. If the neutral beam injection (NBI) is along the same direction of the plasma current, it is called the co-current injection. Otherwise it is called the counter-current injection. Using the above conclusions, we know that, for co-current injection, ions ionized at the low-Ô¨Åeld-side have better conÔ¨Ånement compared with those ionized at the high-Ô¨Åeld-side. Therefore, with the assumption that most neutral beam particles are ionized on the low-Ô¨Åeld-side, co-curent injection is better than counter-current injection in terms of the Ô¨Årst-orbit loss.
