The ∂r∕∂ψ|𝜃,α lines can be understood from another perspective. Examine a family of magnetic ﬁeld lines that start from the low-ﬁeld-side midplane (𝜃 = 0) with the same toroidal angle ϕ = ϕ1 but diﬀerent radial coordinates. These starting points all have the same value of α, which is equal to ϕ. When following these ﬁeld lines to another isosurfce of 𝜃, the intersecting points of these ﬁeld lines with the 𝜃 isosurface will trace out one ∂r∕∂ψ|𝜃,α line. Examine another family of magnetic ﬁeld lines similar to the above but with the starting toroidal angle ϕ = ϕ2. They will trace out another ∂r∕∂ψ|𝜃,α line on the 𝜃 isosurface. Continue the process, we ﬁnally get those lines in Fig. 14b and Fig. 15b.

If the saving associated with using less parallel grid number out-weights the cost associated with using more radial grid number, we obtain a net saving in using the ﬁeld-aligned coordinates.
