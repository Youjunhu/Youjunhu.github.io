9.7 Resonant surface of a perturbation

Equation (275) indicates that, for the diﬀerential equation (271), there is a resonant response to a perturbation ei(m𝜃−nζ) on a magnetic surface with m − nq = 0. Therefore, the magnetic surface with q = m∕n is called the “resonant surface” for the perturbation ei(m𝜃−nζ). The phase change of the perturbation along a magnetic ﬁeld is given by mΔ𝜃 −nΔζ, which can be written as Δ𝜃(m−nq). Since m − nq = 0 on a resonant surface, the above formula indicates that there is no phase change along a magnetic ﬁeld line on a resonant surface, i.e., the parallel wavenumber k∥ is zero on a resonant surface.
