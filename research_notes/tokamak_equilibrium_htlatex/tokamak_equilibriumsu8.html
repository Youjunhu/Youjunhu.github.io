1.8 Non-axisymmetric magnetic perturbations

In the above, the magnetic ﬁeld is assumed to be axisymmetric. With this assumption, the poloidal magnetic ﬁeld (having two components) can be expressed in terms of a single function Ψ. This kind of simpliﬁcation can not be achieved if the axisymmetricity assumption is dropped, i.e., introducing Ψ in the non-axisymmetric case is not very useful because other components of the vector potential (namely AR and AZ) will appear in the expression of the poloidal magnetic ﬁeld.

In the so-called tearing mode theory, a magnetic perturbation (which is non-axisymmetric) is approximately considered as such that its vector potential has only nonzero toroidal component, i.e., δAϕ≠0, δAR ≈ δAZ ≈ 0.
