Abstract

Axisymmetric equilibrium magnetic ﬁeld of a tokamak is the basis for real-time plasma control and analysis of stability and transport. These are my notes when learning tokamak equilibrium theory. To test what I have learned, I have developed two numerical codes, one of which constructs a ﬂux coordinate system with a desired form of Jacobian by using discrete numerical equilibrium data output by the EFIT code, another of which solves the ﬁxed boundary Grad-Shafranov equation in a general coordinate system. These notes serve as a document for the codes. These notes are evolving and are written for my own record (which means that something might be very wrong). Email me if you have any comments.
